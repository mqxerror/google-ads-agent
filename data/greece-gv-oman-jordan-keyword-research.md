## 1. Language strategy — "Arabic or English?" (answer: **both, English-led, per-country weighting**)

### The core truth about this audience
HNW investment-migration is a **globalized, English-jargon category**. Even fully Arabic-speaking Gulf and Levant investors — and the advisors, family offices, and private bankers around them — overwhelmingly search the *industry* terms in **English**: "golden visa", "residency by investment", "greece golden visa", "eu golden visa". These English head terms carry the **highest intent and the deepest reach** in both markets. Arabic volume is real and rising, but it skews **earlier-funnel and broader** ("الإقامة الذهبية" = "golden residency" as a concept) rather than program-specific.

**One thing separates Greece from other programs we've mapped:** the **Greece-named terms actually carry real volume — and even CPC data.** Unlike thin, obscure programs where the country-named keywords all sit at <10 searches, Greece has **genuine brand-search demand** in Oman and Jordan — "golden visa greece" and "greek golden visa" each register measurable volume (10–20/mo per market) *and* return live bid ranges ($0.63–$5.02). That means the Greece head terms aren't just intent-catchers with no reach; there's a real audience already typing Greece by name. Treat that as a **positive**: you can bid on Greece-specific money terms and actually get impressions.

### Recommendation
Run **two parallel Search campaigns from day one** — an **English campaign (primary, ~70% of budget)** and an **Arabic campaign (secondary, ~30%)** — *not* one campaign with two languages mixed. Separating them lets you:
- control budget split by language (English is where ready-to-invest intent lives),
- write ad copy natively in each language (Arabic is not a translation of English — hooks differ),
- read performance cleanly (CPL by language tells you where to shift spend after 2–3 weeks).

Google's **language targeting** targets the *user's browser/Google interface language*, **not the query language** — so the real separation between EN and AR campaigns is driven by the **keyword list** (English keywords vs Arabic keywords), with language targeting set as a secondary signal (see §4).

### Per-country weighting

| | **Oman** | **Jordan** |
|---|---|---|
| Region / profile | Gulf (GCC); very high HNW density per capita; oil-wealth + established business families | Levant; mixed HNW + a large, mobile diaspora and expat/second-nationality community (Syrian-, Palestinian-, Iraqi-origin wealth resident in Amman) |
| Bilingualism / search language | **Highly bilingual**; business + finance conducted in English; investors search English jargon readily | **More Arabic-leaning** in everyday search, but the *investor* segment still uses English terms; strong Arabic long-tail |
| Dominant motive | Portfolio diversification into European real estate, Schengen mobility, family optionality, prestige EU foothold | **Plan B / security in a stable EU country**, Schengen mobility (Jordan's own passport is weak — ~50 visa-free countries — so residents actively seek EU-grade travel freedom), family protection |
| Language mix (recommended) | **~75% English / 25% Arabic** | **~60% English / 40% Arabic** |
| Copy angle emphasis | EU real-estate asset + Schengen mobility + prestige of a European address | **Plan B + family security + Schengen mobility + no minimum stay**; "an EU foothold" resonates strongly |

**Net:** English first for intent capture in **both** countries; a genuine parallel Arabic campaign to catch the meaningful (and less-contested, often cheaper) Arabic demand — weighted a little heavier toward Jordan.

## 2. Keyword clusters (ad-group-ready)

**Conventions used below**
- **Match type:** `[exact]` for proven high-intent money terms; `"phrase"` for controlled discovery. **Do not use broad match** on a new $30/day account — it will burn budget on irrelevant traffic. (Revisit broad + Smart Bidding only after conversion data accrues.)

Gulf/Levant investors mostly type **MSA** for these formal finance terms, so MSA is the backbone; dialect mainly affects softer/colloquial phrasings.

> **Consolidation note (honest at $30/day):** two co-equal campaigns is the *target* design once volume justifies it. On a genuine $30/day beachhead, the deepest signal is in English — if week-1 delivery is thin, **run English-first** and treat Arabic as a small always-on probe rather than a full second campaign, then split into two proper campaigns once conversions accrue. Keep the 70/30 split table above as the design; scale into it.

> **Geo option (optional, later):** if Oman and Jordan diverge sharply on CPL or you want per-country budgets/copy, split each language campaign into two (…·Oman and …·Jordan). Start unified to gather data on $30/day; split once one geo clearly out/under-performs. ### Bidding
