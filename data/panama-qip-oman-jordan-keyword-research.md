## 1. Language strategy — "Arabic or English?" (answer: **both, English-led, per-country weighting**)

### The core truth about this audience
HNW investment-migration is a **globalized, English-jargon category**. Even fully Arabic-speaking Gulf and Levant investors — and the advisors, family offices, and private bankers around them — overwhelmingly search the *industry* terms in **English**: "golden visa", "residency by investment", "citizenship by investment", "second passport", "Panama golden visa". These English head terms carry the **highest intent and the deepest reach** in both markets. Arabic volume is real and rising, but it skews **earlier-funnel and broader** ("الجنسية عن طريق الاستثمار" = "citizenship by investment" as a concept) rather than program-specific.

### Recommendation
Run **two parallel Search campaigns from day one** — an **English campaign (primary, ~70% of budget)** and an **Arabic campaign (secondary, ~30%)** — *not* one campaign with two languages mixed. Separating them lets you:
- control budget split by language (English is where ready-to-invest intent lives),
- write ad copy natively in each language (Arabic is not a translation of English — hooks differ),
- read performance cleanly (CPL by language tells you where to shift spend after 2–3 weeks).

Google's **language targeting** targets the *user's browser/Google interface language*, **not the query language** — so the real separation between EN and AR campaigns is driven by the **keyword list** (English keywords vs Arabic keywords), with language targeting set as a secondary signal (see §4).

### Per-country weighting

| | **Oman** | **Jordan** |
|---|---|---|
| Region / profile | Gulf (GCC); very high HNW density per capita; oil-wealth + established business families | Levant; mixed HNW + a large, mobile diaspora and expat/second-nationality community (Syrian-, Palestinian-, Iraqi-origin wealth resident in Amman) |
| Bilingualism / search language | **Highly bilingual**; business + finance conducted in English; investors search English jargon readily | **More Arabic-leaning** in everyday search, but the *investor* segment still uses English terms; strong Arabic long-tail |
| Dominant motive | Portfolio diversification, mobility, tax-efficient base, family optionality | **Plan B / security in a stable country**, passport mobility (Jordan's own passport is weak — ~50 visa-free countries — so residents actively seek a *better* passport elsewhere), family protection |
| Language mix (recommended) | **~75% English / 25% Arabic** | **~60% English / 40% Arabic** |
| Copy angle emphasis | Tax + mobility + prestige asset (tower/hotel co-ownership) | **Plan B + family security + mobility + speed (30 days)**; "stable USD economy" resonates strongly |

**Net:** English first for intent capture in **both** countries; a genuine parallel Arabic campaign to catch the meaningful (and less-contested, often cheaper) Arabic demand — weighted a little heavier toward Jordan.

## 2. Keyword clusters (ad-group-ready)

**Conventions used below**
- **Match type:** `[exact]` for proven high-intent money terms; `"phrase"` for controlled discovery. **Do not use broad match** on a new $30/day account — it will burn budget on irrelevant traffic. (Revisit broad + Smart Bidding only after conversion data accrues.)

**Networks:** **Search only.** Turn **OFF** "Search partners" and **OFF** "Display Network" for a new precision campaign — both dilute a narrow HNW audience on a small budget. Revisit Search Partners after you have clean data.

**Ad schedule:** all-week to start. After 2 weeks, check hour/day performance; Gulf/Levant business hours + evenings typically index well. Don't hand-restrict before data.

### Bidding

- **Both campaigns: `Maximize Conversions`** (no tCPA yet — a brand-new campaign has zero conversion history to feed a target). Let it learn on the primary conversion action.
- **Primary conversion action:** the LP's main lead event (consultation request / guide download / call). Ensure it's imported as a **Google Ads conversion** and, ideally, that **Enhanced Conversions** are on (the LP already runs GTM `GTM-K6864NBH` per account tracking notes — confirm the QIP LP fires a distinct lead conversion so this campaign is measured on *its own* leads, not blended with other Mercan campaigns).
- **After ~15–30 conversions:** graduate to **tCPA** (or **Maximize Conversions with a target CPA**) once you know a real cost-per-lead. Until then, tCPA will starve delivery.
- **AG4 (Panama-specific):** if using portfolio/manual levers later, bias bids **up** here — these are the readiest buyers and lowest volume, so you can afford aggressive bids on tiny traffic.

## 5. Ad-copy angles per cluster (EN + AR)

**Grounding rules (hard):**
- Every claim traces to §0. **No invented stats, no fabricated visa-free counts, no made-up timelines.**
- **No third-party hotel-brand names in ad copy** (legal caution). The hotel is described **generically** — "a five-star hotel & casino residence", "executive suites in a landmark tower". (Pullman/Accor and Santa Maria names live on the LP/consultation, not in ads.) — consistent with the account's "no brand names in ads" rule.
- **Citizenship honesty:** Panama is **residency now → citizenship eligibility after 5 years**. Never imply instant citizenship/passport. Frame as "permanent residency in ~30 days, a clear path to citizenship after 5 years."

## 6. Measurement note

- **Volumes & CPCs → Google Keyword Planner.** Do the pull with **geo = Oman + Jordan** and **language = English and Arabic**, separately, so you can size each language market. Nothing in this doc asserts a volume figure — all keyword sizing is a Planner task.
- **Expect Panama-specific terms (cluster D) to be low-volume** in both markets. The **reach comes from the head terms** — golden visa / residency by investment / citizenship by investment (clusters A/B/C) — while **cluster D carries the intent**. Budget-and-bid accordingly: broad head terms for volume, Panama terms bid up for conversion.
- **CPC reality:** this category has **premium CPCs** globally; a $30/day budget will produce **modest daily click volume**. Judge success on **cost-per-qualified-lead**, not clicks or impressions.
- **Conversion tracking:** confirm the QIP LP fires its **own** distinct lead conversion (consultation / guide / call) into Google Ads — ideally via the existing GTM container (`GTM-K6864NBH`) plus a dedicated conversion action for this campaign, with **Enhanced Conversions** on. Measuring this campaign on *its own* leads (not blended with other Mercan campaigns) is what lets Maximize Conversions optimize correctly.
- **Bidding graduation:** start **Maximize Conversions**; move to **tCPA** only after ~15–30 conversions establish a real CPL.
- **Review cadence:** Search Terms report 2×/week (harvest negatives + new keywords); at **day ~21** decide the 70/30 EN/AR split re-baseline and whether to scale the $30/day budget, based on cost-per-qualified-lead by language and by country.
