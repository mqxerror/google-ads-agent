## THE 3-ANGLE FRAMEWORK (non-negotiable structure)

When drafting prompts from a landing-page brief, ALWAYS produce three distinct prompts, one per angle. Operator picks the one that fits the campaign's current creative rotation.

### Angle 1 — PROBLEM-LED  ("Plan B" / urgency / security)
Visualizes the unsolved-problem state or the moment of relief AFTER solving it.

- Subject in a state of consideration / decision / quiet resolve
- Setting carries hint of geopolitical uncertainty or family stewardship without being on-the-nose (no news ticker on screen, no protest footage)
- Tone: contemplative, deliberate, weight-of-decision
- Avoid: anxious / fearful expressions, dystopian palette, doomscroll aesthetic

**Example shape**: "A man in his 50s stands at a floor-to-ceiling window of a high-rise apartment overlooking a city at dusk, holding a tumbler of whiskey, watching the lights come on across the skyline. Editorial photography, 50mm lens, shallow depth of field, warm window-light from inside, indigo sky outside. Quiet, deliberative, weight of a decision being made."

### Angle 2 — ASPIRATIONAL  (the lived outcome)
Visualizes the future state the investor is buying. Not a fantasy — a believable upgrade.

- Subject inhabiting the outcome (the residence, the location, the freedom)
- Setting is the program's specific country / city — never generic "luxury beach"
- Tone: composed, settled, ownership stance — NOT excited / euphoric / vacation-mode
- Avoid: clinking-champagne-glasses, sunset-jump silhouettes, group-of-friends-toasting

**Example shape**: "A composed couple in their 40s stands on a high-rise terrace overlooking Panama City's skyline at dusk, warm coral light catching their faces, the city's towers glowing beneath an indigo sky. 35mm, shallow depth of field, restrained styling. Editorial photography, calm and quietly aspirational without feeling transactional."

### Angle 3 — SOCIAL-PROOF  (institutional trust / longevity)
Visualizes credibility, regulation, longevity — the firm's authority signals. Pinned-claim grounded.

- Subject is the firm's authority surface (advisor in consultation, signed document, regulated framing) OR the investor in a moment that conveys "this is real, this is regulated"
- Setting carries professional weight (boardroom, advisor office, document signing) but NEVER bureaucratic / DMV / immigration-counter aesthetic
- Tone: clinical, precise, institutional — the visual equivalent of "OISC-regulated, 37 years serving diaspora"
- Avoid: any setting that reads as a government office or visa-application counter

**Example shape**: "Close-up of two hands across a polished wood table — one resting on a leather portfolio, the other gesturing toward a printed property prospectus laid open. Soft directional window light from camera-left, 50mm lens, shallow depth of field, restrained palette of olive and tan. Editorial documentary photography, conveys the calm authority of a regulated advisory practice."
