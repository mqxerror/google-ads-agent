> **Data provenance.** All search-volume and CPC figures in this document were pulled from **Google Keyword Planner via the Google Ads API (v23)** — `KeywordPlanIdeaService.GenerateKeywordHistoricalMetrics`, account 7178239091 — for geographies **Oman (2512)** and **Jordan (2400)**, languages **English (1000)** and **Arabic (1019)**, network **Google Search**, over the **last 12 months**. > **How to read the numbers.** Keyword Planner reports volume as **rounded ranges** (10 / 20 / 30 / 40 / 50…), not exact counts. Treat every figure here as **directional sizing, not a guarantee** of clicks or conversions. Many terms in these two markets return **`<10`** (below Keyword Planner's reporting floor of ~10 searches/month) — that is genuinely how much search there is. These are **small, high-value niche markets**, exactly as the strategy work predicted; the low volumes are the honest, expected picture, not a data error. The value of this category is in *who* searches (high-net-worth investors), not *how many*.

## 1. Executive summary

There is real, capturable demand in Oman and Jordan for a **second residency and an EU foothold** — two under-served Gulf and Levant markets with high per-capita wealth, strong mobility motives, and (for Jordan especially) an active search for Schengen access and a stable, EU-based Plan B. This campaign puts Mercan's Greece Golden Visa in front of that audience on Google Search, in **both English and Arabic**.

**The honest reality, stated up front:**

- **Total addressable demand is roughly 1,080 searches/month** across every keyword we researched, both countries, both languages (deduplicated — each unique term counted once). That is a **small, premium niche**, not a high-volume market.
- **Greece-specific search terms are low but genuinely present.** Unlike the Panama research (where every *Panama-named* term returned `<10`), Greece-named terms *do* register measurable volume — *golden visa greece* and *greek golden visa* each report **10 / 20 (Oman / Jordan)** and, notably, **returned real CPC bid data**. That is a positive signal: there is genuine Greece brand-search demand in these markets, not just generic-category curiosity.
- **So the strategy splits reach from intent.** The **broad head terms** — *golden visa*, *residency by investment*, *eu golden visa*, and their Arabic equivalents *الإقامة الذهبية* / *الفيزا الذهبية* — carry the **reach** (this is where the searches actually are). The **Greece-specific terms** carry the **intent** (whoever types them is already looking at Greece). We bid to capture both: broad terms for volume, Greece terms bid up for conversion.
- **English leads.** Investment-migration is a globalised, English-jargon category; even Arabic-speaking Gulf and Levant investors (and the bankers and advisors around them) overwhelmingly search the industry terms in English. English carries the deeper intent and the wider reach in *both* countries; Arabic is real, meaningful, and often cheaper, but broader and earlier-funnel.
- **The CTA lever is a real, already-happened cost rise.** Greece's 2024–2025 reforms *raised and tiered* the entry thresholds (the old flat €250K became €400K / €800K tiers, with €250K surviving only via conversion projects). That is a legitimate "costs have already risen — the lower-entry routes are narrowing, act while they remain" hook, and it should headline sitelinks and callouts.

**Market shape at a glance (deduplicated monthly searches):**

| Split | Monthly searches | Read |
|---|---|---|
| **English** | **830** | Where ready-to-invest intent + reach concentrate |
| **Arabic** | **250** | Real, broader/earlier demand; often cheaper CPCs |
| **Oman** | **510** | Near-even with Jordan |
| **Jordan** | **570** | Slightly ahead of Oman; strong EU-foothold motive |
| **Total (unique)** | **≈ 1,080 / mo** | The full addressable market, both languages, both countries |

**Bottom line:** this is a **precision, high-value-per-lead** campaign, not a volume play. Success is measured in **cost-per-qualified-lead**, not clicks or impressions.

## 2. Recommendations

### 2.1 Campaign structure

| Setting | Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Channel** | **Search only** | Precision audience on a small budget |
| **Search Partners** | **OFF** | Dilutes a narrow HNW audience; revisit once data is clean |
| **Display Network** | **OFF** | Same — no display for a precision search test |
| **Campaigns (target design)** | An **English campaign** + a parallel **Arabic campaign** (per the strategy work) — **but at the launch budget, consolidate to one English-first campaign; see §3** | Clean per-language budget control + native ad copy + clean CPL read *once budget supports it* |
| **Geo targeting** | **Oman + Jordan**, location option set to **"Presence: people in your targeted locations"** (NOT "presence *or interest*") | We want people physically in Oman/Jordan, not everyone worldwide merely interested |
| **Language targeting** | English on the EN campaign, Arabic on the AR campaign — **with the caveat that Google language targeting keys off the user's browser/Google-interface language, not the query.** The real EN/AR split is enforced by the **keyword list.** | Do not rely on language targeting alone to separate the two |
| **Devices** | Start all devices; monitor mobile vs desktop (HNW research often starts on mobile, converts on desktop/phone-call) | Don't cut mobile early |
| **Ad schedule** | All-week to start; review hour/day after 2 weeks | Let data decide, not assumptions |

### 2.2 Ad groups — six, mirroring the six keyword clusters

Each campaign is organised into **6 tightly-themed ad groups**, one per intent cluster, so every ad speaks to exactly the searcher's mindset. Because Greece is a *residency-by-investment via real-estate* program, the cluster mix differs from other programs — the ordering below reflects that:

| Ad group | Cluster | Priority | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AG1 · Greece Golden Visa** | A | **Highest intent** | Greece-named category head; **bid up**, `[exact]`, sharpest ads — and it carries real reach too |
| **AG2 · Residency by Investment** | B | High reach | The precise category label; research→ready bridge |
| **AG3 · Property / Real-estate** | C | **High intent** | "buy property → residency" — the mechanism of the program; **bid up**, sharp ads |
| **AG4 · EU Residency / Schengen** | D | Mobility / EU foothold | Frame as EU residence + Schengen access — **residency, never citizenship** |
| **AG5 · Investment Migration / Investor Visa** | E | **High intent** | Greece-named investor-migration terms; **bid up**, `[exact]`, sharp ads |
| **AG6 · Comparison / Best-Cheapest-Fastest** | F | Commercial shopper | Lead with EU access + tiered entry from €250K (conversion) / €400K |

> **AG1, AG3 and AG5 are the money clusters** — the Greece-named, high-intent terms. Give them `[exact]` match, the sharpest copy, and **bid up**; they are the readiest buyers, and (for AG1) they also carry genuine reach.

### 2.3 Bidding

- **Start on `Maximize Conversions`** on both campaigns — **no target CPA yet.** A brand-new campaign has zero conversion history to feed a target, so tCPA would starve delivery.
- **Primary conversion action:** the LP's main lead event (consultation request / guide download / tap-to-call). Confirm the Greece landing page fires **its own distinct lead conversion** into Google Ads — ideally through the existing GTM container (`GTM-K6864NBH`) with **Enhanced Conversions** on — so this campaign is optimised on *its own* leads, not blended with other Mercan activity.
- **Graduate to tCPA after ~15–30 conversions**, once a real cost-per-lead is known.
- **AG1 / AG3 / AG5 (Greece-named):** bias bids **up** — these are the readiest buyers at low volume, so aggressive bids on tiny traffic are affordable.

### 2.4 Negative keywords (protect a narrow budget)

Apply a shared negative list at **campaign level** on both campaigns. The goal is to strip adjacent-but-worthless traffic that never invests six figures in EU real estate. Summarised (the full EN + AR sets are held in the strategy file):

- **Jobs / work:** jobs, vacancy, hiring, salary, work visa, work permit, employment — AR: وظائف, عمل, تأشيرة عمل, راتب
- **Tourism / travel:** tourist, tourism, vacation, hotel booking, flights, airbnb, things to do, islands holiday — AR: سياحة, سياحية, فندق, طيران
- **Study:** student, study, scholarship, university, school — AR: دراسة, منحة
- **Retire / lifestyle:** retire, retirement, retiree, pension — (retiree relocation is not the Golden Visa buyer)
- **Cheap-relocation / cost-of-living:** cost of living, rent, apartment for rent, cheap living, budget, digital nomad — AR: تكلفة المعيشة, إيجار, رخيص للسكن
- **Free / humanitarian:** free, grant, asylum, refugee, embassy appointment — AR: مجاني, لجوء, لاجئ
- **Stray:** map, weather, greek language course, translation — AR: ترجمة

> **Ongoing hygiene:** run the **Search Terms report twice weekly** for the first month and harvest negatives aggressively — a small budget cannot absorb waste. Watch especially for other-country golden-visa searches: decide per term whether to exclude or redirect into AG6 (comparison).

### 2.5 The rising-cost / narrowing-entry lever

Greece's real-estate thresholds have **already risen and tiered** in the 2024–2025 reforms: the old flat **€250,000** entry is now **€400,000** across most of Greece and **€800,000** in prime zones (Athens/Attica, Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini, and islands over ~3,100 population), with the **€250,000 route surviving only via conversion projects.** That is a legitimate, factual "costs have gone up and the low-entry routes are narrowing — act while they remain available" hook. Keep it factual and soft; frame it as **rising cost / narrowing entry**, never as a countdown to a specific future date:

- **Sitelink (EN):** "Secure the €250K Conversion Route" → deep-link to the LP consultation
- **Sitelink (AR):** "قبل ارتفاع تكاليف الإقامة" ("before residency costs rise")
- **Callout:** "Entry from €250K via conversion — while routes remain" / "Thresholds have risen — act on the lower tiers"

## 3. Budget plan — $30/day (a beachhead, not a scale budget)

**The recommendation: at $30/day, run English-first across BOTH countries as a single campaign to gather signal. Do not fragment into four cells.**

This is a **deliberate departure** from the strategy document's day-one 70/30 English/Arabic two-campaign split. That parallel-Arabic structure is the right move — **once budget reaches roughly $75–150/day.** At $30/day it spreads spend too thin to learn. The rationale is the math below.

### 3.1 What $30/day buys (one English-first campaign, both countries)

This is a **premium-CPC category** — golden-visa / residency-by-investment clicks are among the most expensive in paid search. The actual CPC *paid* usually sits below the top-of-page bid, so we model an **effective-CPC band**:

| Effective CPC | Scenario | Clicks/day at full $30 | Clicks/month (~30d) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **$2.50** | Optimistic — Jordan / Arabic-leaning, cheaper clicks | **12.0** | ~360 |
| **$4.00** | Mid — blended English | **7.5** | ~225 |
| **$6.00** | Conservative — premium English, Oman-weighted | **5.0** | ~150 |

→ Roughly **150–360 clicks/month**. Modest by design — enough to read direction and cost-per-lead, not to chase scale.

### 3.2 Why NOT split into four cells

Splitting $30/day across four cells — EN-Oman, EN-Jordan, AR-Oman, AR-Jordan — leaves **$7.50/day each**:

| Per-cell budget | @ $6 CPC | @ $4 CPC |
|---|---|---|
| **$7.50/day** | **1.2 clicks/day** | **1.9 clicks/day** |

That is **below the ~2 clicks/day floor** a Maximize-Conversions campaign needs to exit the learning phase. **Splitting starves every cell** — four campaigns that each learn nothing beats one campaign that learns. Consolidate now; layer Arabic and per-country geo splits only when budget scales.

### 3.3 Scaled scenarios (when to graduate)

| Daily budget | Split | Effective CPC | Clicks/day | Clicks/month | Milestone |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **$30** | EN-first, one campaign, both countries | $4.00 | ~7.5 | ~225 | Beachhead — prove CPL & direction |
| **$75** | EN **$52** + AR **$23** | $4.00 | ~19 | ~560 | Split Arabic into its own campaign |
| **$150** | EN **$105** + AR **$45** | $4.00 | ~38 | ~1,125 | Enough conversion volume to graduate to tCPA + consider per-country geo splits |

> **Read this as a beachhead.** $30/day is built to answer *"what does a qualified Greece Golden Visa lead cost in these markets, and does English or Arabic convert cheaper?"* — not to deliver volume. Make the scale-up decision around **day ~21**, judged on **cost-per-qualified-lead**, not click count.
